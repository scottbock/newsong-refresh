What to Expect

At New Song, you will find a warm, friendly atmosphere with no expectation to look a certain way. Dress casually or dress up. We are more interested in your readiness to meet with God than with your ability to impress us. In our worship service, you can expect Christ-Centered preaching that seeks to explain the meaning of Bible passages and how they apply to your everyday life. While some sermons may be topical, most will be a part of a larger sermon series based on books of the Bible. Our worship music is contemporary and features new songs, but also includes modernized hymns from the past. Our name “New Song” reveals how much we like to sing and how we have been blessed with talented musicians to lead us in worship. Our service includes elements which have been an important part of the worship by God’s people throughout the ages (Call to Worship, Confession of Sin, Assurance of Pardon, Corporate Prayer, etc.). We hope you will visit us soon and experience the presence of God in worship.

About New Song

At New Song Church, you’ll find welcoming people with a heart for loving others. As followers of Jesus, we find life and hope in Him. New Song strives to be a place where the Gospel is real and where we experience Jesus’ work of making all things new. You’ll find authentic relationships where you can know others and be known. Why the name New Song? First, we love music! You’ll find songs of worship which glorify the Lord and his greatness. Second, we believe that the gospel of grace produces new songs in our lives. These testimonies of God’s goodness reveal how God transforms us and put us in a community where we blend together our songs into a symphony “to the praise of his glorious grace.”

We Believe

the Bible is the written word of God, inspired by the Holy Spirit and without error in the original manuscripts. The Bible is the revelation of God’s truth and is infallible and authoritative in all matters of faith and practice.

We Believe

in the Holy Trinity. There is one God, who exists eternally in three persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.

We Believe

that all are sinners and totally unable to save themselves from God’s displeasure, except by His mercy.

We Believe

God has shown His mercy by sending Jesus Christ to save sinners by His grace alone, not based on any human works.

We Believe

Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God, who through His perfect life, sacrificial death, and resurrection atoned for and conquered the sin of all who will trust in Him, alone, for salvation.

We Believe

God is gracious and faithful to His people according to His covenant promises.

We Believe

the Holy Spirit indwells God’s people and gives them the desire, ability, and wisdom to trust Christ and follow Him.

We Believe

Jesus will return, bodily and visibly, to judge all people from all ages and to receive His people to Himself.

The PCA

The Presbyterian Church in America (PCA) was formed in 1973 to be a denomination that is “Faithful to the Scriptures, True to the Reformed Faith, and Obedient to the Great Commission.” The PCA is an evangelical denomination in that we proclaim the gospel of salvation through faith in Jesus Christ. The word “evangelical” comes from the Greek word meaning “good news.” We desire all people to trust in the saving work of Jesus and enjoy eternal life in him. The PCA is a reformed denomination in that we believe in the biblical truth proclaimed during the Protestant Reformation. The Word of God, rather than tradition, is the only guide for the Church. God alone saves through his immeasurable mercy and according to his sovereign plan. We believe the system of doctrine taught in the Bible is summarized well in the Westminster Confession of Faith with the Larger and Shorter Catechisms. The PCA is a Presbyterian denomination in that we have a representative form of church government. A local church is governed by a “Session” comprised of elders (i.e., “presbyters”) elected by the members of the congregation. Pastors and representatives of local churches in a region form a “Presbytery.” Representatives of Presbyteries and local churches meet annually at a “General Assembly.” The General Assembly Committees and Agencies help local churches combine their efforts and resources to advance God’s Kingdom more effectively.
